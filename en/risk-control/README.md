1. Risks Related to Digital Assets

- The price and value of any investment in digital asset products and the returns thereon (if any) may fluctuate and may move against your interests. The price of individual digital asset products may fluctuate downward and, in certain circumstances, may become worthless. The inherent risk of buying and selling/investing in digital asset products is that such trading may result in losses rather than profits.

- Digital assets are not legal tender and are not backed by any government or commodities such as gold or silver. There is no central bank that can take corrective action to protect the value of Bitcoin and/or Ethereum and/or any other digital asset in a crisis, or issue more digital assets. Instead, Bitcoin, Ethereum, and any other digital assets are autonomous within a global network and are largely unregulated. Traders place their trust in a digital, decentralized, and partially anonymous system that relies on peer-to-peer networking and cryptography to maintain its integrity.

- Transactions in digital assets may be irreversible, and accordingly, losses resulting from fraudulent or accidental transactions may not be recoverable;

- Certain digital asset transactions should be considered as having been made when recorded on the public ledger, and not necessarily on the date or time you initiated the transaction;

- The value of digital assets may be derived from the continued willingness of market participants to exchange fiat currency or digital assets for digital assets, and the disappearance of the market for a particular digital asset could result in the permanent and total loss of value of that digital asset;

- The nature of digital assets may lead to an increased risk of fraud or cyberattacks, and may mean that technical difficulties experienced by the Platform could result in the inability to access or use your digital assets;

- Digital asset trading is susceptible to irrational (or rational) bubbles or loss of confidence, which could cause a collapse in demand relative to supply. For example, confidence in Bitcoin could collapse due to unexpected changes by software developers or others, government crackdowns, the emergence of a superior competitive alternative cryptocurrency, deflationary or inflationary spirals. Confidence could also collapse if the anonymity of the system is compromised, if funds are lost or stolen, or if hackers or governments can prevent any transaction from settling.

- Depending on the applicable laws, regulations, or rules of your jurisdiction, the deposits in your account may not be regarded as deposits.

- Digital assets in your account are not protected by applicable deposit insurance.
